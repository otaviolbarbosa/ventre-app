# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Healthcare professionals — obstetras, enfermeiras, and doulas — all first-class users with no dominant role. They operate in multidisciplinary teams, coordinating care for pregnant patients across the gestational journey. Their context: busy clinical workflows, often switching between screens during or between appointments. They are skilled, time-constrained, and bear real responsibility for patient outcomes.

## Product Purpose

Ventre is a maternal healthcare management platform for multidisciplinary teams. It consolidates patient records, gestational tracking, appointment scheduling, team collaboration, billing, and AI-assisted insights in one place. Success looks like a team that spends less time on administrative coordination and more time on quality care — with every professional always in context about a patient's history, needs, and next steps.

## Brand Personality

Cuidado, clareza, confiança.

Warm and caring, but precise and trustworthy — like a good clinician. The emotional register is calm confidence, not clinical sterility and not consumer warmth. It's the feeling of a well-run practice: organized, human, and serious about the people it serves.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS (cream + blue)**: Notion/Linear/Intercom aesthetic — clean but cold and faceless. Ventre has a specific domain and human stakes; it should feel like it was built for this work.
- **Clinical white (hospital UI)**: Sterile, institutional healthcare software. Maternal care is about life and relationship, not bureaucracy.
- **Dark-mode dev tools**: Terminal-native, high-contrast, code-editor aesthetic. This is a product used by clinicians, not engineers.

## Design Principles

1. **Cuidado visível em cada detalhe.** Warmth isn't a color — it's a choice in every interaction. Empty states, error messages, and loading screens carry the same care as the core feature. Nothing should feel abandoned.

2. **Clareza acima de completude.** Multidisciplinary teams are busy. Surfaces communicate the essential at a glance before offering depth. No screen should require reading to understand its purpose.

3. **Confiança construída, não declarada.** Trust comes from consistent, calm behavior — not from security badges or reassuring copy. Data feels safe because the UI is organized and never anxious.

4. **Respeito pela competência.** These are skilled professionals. The UI reduces cognitive load without being patronizing. It guides without hand-holding, informs without lecturing.

5. **Humanidade na medicina.** This is maternal care — the product should reflect the weight and joy of that work. Interactions feel human because the people using them are human, caring for humans.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Color contrast ratios ≥ 4.5:1 for normal text, ≥ 3:1 for large text and UI components. Keyboard navigation throughout. Screen-reader-friendly labels and ARIA where native semantics fall short. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all non-essential animations.
